## Karpathy Read-Order Rule

The vault follows the Karpathy Hot/Index/Wiki working-memory pattern. Read in this order, every session, no exceptions:

1. **Hot first** — `wiki/hot.md` is the working-memory cache (≤500 words). What's recent, what's blocking, where to start. Updated every meaningful session, overwritten in place (not appended).
2. **Index second** — `wiki/index.md` is the navigation map. Section-grouped wikilinks to every note in the vault. Read after hot to find the specific note you need.
3. **Relevant note third** — open the wikilinked note that answers your question. Trust the inbound wikilinks; the vault is structured so every note is reachable in 2-3 hops from `hot.md`.

Cross-project rule for any Codex, Claude, or agent session pointed at this vault: `Read wiki/hot.md first (~500w), then wiki/index.md, then domain _index.md, then individual pages.`

## How To Use This Vault

1. Read `wiki/hot.md` first.
2. Read `wiki/index.md` to find the right note.
3. Open the relevant flow, page brief, or decision note.
4. Read the root `shipping-rules.md` before any implementation- or publication-impacting decision.
5. Before creating, refreshing, merging, or pruning any URL, complete `[[SERP-First Content Creation Gate]]` and update the keyword/URL ledger.
6. Treat `.raw/` as immutable source material. Do not edit raw files unless deliberately refreshing a source.
7. Update `wiki/hot.md`, `wiki/index.md`, `wiki/log.md`, and `wiki/overview.md` after meaningful work.
8. Update `.raw/.manifest.json` whenever raw sources are added or refreshed.

## Operating Rules

- This brain is advisory until **Day 0** ([[Day 0 Measurement Access Gate]]) closes — every recommendation downstream of Day 0 is hypothesis until baselines land.
- Do not promise specific traffic numbers, guaranteed rankings, or fixed recovery/launch dates. Outcomes depend on prune quality, content quality, off-site signals, and Google's update cadence — most of which is not under our control.
- Preserve site/domain unless {{owner}} confirms a change: `{{site_url}}`.
- Use FLOW: Find -> Leverage -> Optimize -> Win. The Find step is the [[Keyword Strategy Framework]] research output plus GSC loss-query reconciliation once GSC access lands.
- Every primary keyword maps to exactly one canonical owner URL. If a planned keyword or same-intent variant already has an owner, refresh/merge into that owner instead of publishing a competing page.
- The active business-type overlay is `[[Business Type Overlay]]` — read it to understand the vertical-specific revenue, content priority, measurement, and anti-pattern rules. Overlays sit in `wiki/business-types/` and are loaded by the skill at scaffold time.
- No mass AI-generated content. E-E-A-T requires real author identity, real evidence, and dated proof of practice or research. AI is for assistance (outlines, edits, schema, draft polish), not byline content.
- Review and citation outreach must be neutral: no incentivized reviews, no requested wording, no link-buying, no PBNs.
- Preserve evidence (file paths, URL screenshots, GSC export rows, archived SERPs) for any claim that affects implementation.
- Keep `wiki/log.md` newest-first, append-only. Add a dated note for strategy, source, and validation changes — never edit past entries.
- **Cred safety:** never expose credentials, GSC OAuth tokens, ad-network auth, DataForSEO API keys, or CMS-admin passwords in wiki notes or reports. Use environment variables; rotate after one-off pulls.
- Do not treat the 30-day sprint ([[30-Day Sprint]]) as a ranking guarantee. Treat it as a high-intensity diagnose -> prune -> rebuild -> measure cycle.

## Shipping Rules

- `shipping-rules.md` (vault root) is the release standard for this vault.
- Read first, write second, verify third.
- Every release-impacting slice (page edit, prune, redirect, schema change, ad placement change, lead-form change) needs an owner, verifier, acceptance criteria, validation evidence, and rollback/undo plan before work starts.
- Agent findings are advisory until independently verified against files, GSC exports, live SERP checks, or {{owner}}'s confirmation.
- Do not mark a content brief, prune decision, technical fix, image workflow, schema change, monetization placement, or measurement setup as ready when required evidence is missing.

## Raw Sources

Raw source copies live in `.raw/`. The manifest is `.raw/.manifest.json` and follows the `claude-obsidian:wiki-ingest` schema (`schema_version`, `vault`, `site_under_audit`, `manifest_owner`, `last_updated`, `sources`). Each entry tracks `hash`, `ingested_at`, `pages_created`, `pages_updated`. Raw files are immutable — refresh by adding a new dated copy, do not overwrite.
