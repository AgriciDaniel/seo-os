# Seasonal Keyword Playbook

If {{niche}} has cyclical search demand, this playbook turns the cycle into a refresh and publish calendar. The single most important framing: refreshing or publishing 4-6 weeks BEFORE the peak window captures the rising tide. Refreshing during or after the peak leaves money on the table and reads as reactive content.

If {{niche}} is non-seasonal (e.g., most B2B SaaS, most ongoing services), the active business-type overlay should mark this playbook as N/A and point to the equivalent always-on cadence.

## Why Season Matters (in seasonal niches)

- Search demand follows the underlying activity, not the calendar quarter.
- A peak-season guide refreshed after the peak window is wrong; the demand wave has passed.
- Year-over-year comparisons are the only honest measure in this niche — month-over-month deltas are misleading because the underlying demand swings 10x or more between peak and off-season.
- Pages that compete for seasonal intent must be visibly fresh (datePublished and dateModified updated, content references the upcoming season) before the peak.

## Refresh Calendar

Build a year-round refresh calendar that schedules each seasonal page 4-6 weeks ahead of its peak. Refresh = update datePublished, update dateModified, verify time-sensitive facts against current canonical sources, swap in current-year proof where possible, add a recent practice note.

## Measurement Note

Year-over-year is the only honest measure. When reporting on a seasonal page's performance, compare May 2026 to May 2025, not to April 2026. Month-over-month in seasonal niches is a noise generator that produces false signals.

## Source of Truth

The canonical source for time-sensitive facts is niche-specific (regulator, industry body, manufacturer schedule, conference calendar — depending on niche). The active business-type overlay names the canonical source(s); refresh cycles re-verify against that source on every refresh.
